# Underground Rail ÔÇö Design Proposal

Design-expert response to George's 2026-06-18 report: *"the underground portions of the
lightrail need design expert assistance for both animation and line/coordinate tracking when
undergroundÔÇª the current animation doesn't make sense."* This document is the design half of
V4-PLAN Batch 5's "CONFIRMED BROKEN" note; it specifies what to build and feeds the Batch 3
timetable-paced prediction work. It is grounded in a read of the current tree, not a guess at it.

---

## 1. Why it looks broken today

The current rail stack has no concept of "underground." `pi-setup/get-rail-osm.ps1` queries
Overpass for `railway=light_rail` ways and then, at the line `if ($el.tags.tunnel -eq "yes") {
continue }`, **deliberately discards every tunnel way**. What lands in `web/src/display/render/
rail.ts` is therefore `RAIL_SEGMENTS: [number, number][][]` ÔÇö a bag of *disconnected above-ground
polylines, one per OSM way, in arbitrary order, with the tunnel spans simply missing*. `RailLayer.ts`
draws each segment independently (`ensureProjected` projects them into a per-view cache and `draw`
strokes them with no notion of which segment connects to which, or which line a segment belongs to).
There is no ordered, continuous, line-keyed path anywhere in the client. `TRAIN_LINES` in `trains.ts`
*does* have an ordered stop list, but only for the 2 Line (East Link), which happens to be entirely
above ground ÔÇö so the timetable simulator has never had to confront a tunnel.

The live train is even blinder. `internal/feed/rail.go` forwards raw OBA `position.lat/lon` fixes;
`livetrains.ts` stores each vehicle and, every frame, eases its shown `lat/lon` toward the latest
poll target with a simple exponential filter (`POS_TAU = 1.6 s`). `TrainLayer.ts` then projects that
eased lat/lon directly to screen and draws a bead with a comet tail. When a train enters the
Downtown Seattle Transit Tunnel, Beacon Hill, or the U-DistrictÔåÆNorthgate bore, OBA stops sending
fresh fixes (no GPS underground). Three failure modes follow directly from the code:

1. **Freeze, then teleport.** `livetrains.ts` keeps easing toward the *last* target, so the bead
   coasts to the last surface fix and stops dead on the map ÔÇö often floating over a portal or over
   open ground above the tunnel. When the train re-acquires GPS one or two stations later, a new
   target lands far away and the ease (or the `STALE_S = 90` / `DROP_S = 150` fade) makes it lurch
   or pop across the gap.
2. **Straight-line shortcut.** Because easing is in raw lat/lon, even a partial fix pulls the bead
   along a chord that cuts across the city, ignoring the curved tunnel alignment (there is no
   geometry to follow ÔÇö the tunnel polyline was thrown away).
3. **Wrong-place surfacing / ghost beads.** A train held 90ÔÇô150 s underground starts to desaturate
   and fade (`fade` in `liveTrains()`), so a train that is simply in a tunnel reads identically to a
   train whose feed died ÔÇö the calm "this is stale" grammar fires on a perfectly healthy train.

So the two problems George names are really one missing primitive (an ordered, tunnel-inclusive,
line-keyed polyline) plus one missing motion model (advance along that polyline by *timetable*, not
by easing a stale GPS point). Everything below builds those two things and the calm visual language
that rides on them.

## 2. Coordinate / line tracking underground

### 2.1 The data structure: `RAIL_LINES`

Replace the order-free `RAIL_SEGMENTS` with an ordered, line-keyed, tunnel-aware structure. Add to
`rail.ts` (generated by the script; see ┬º2.3) alongside the existing `RailStation`:

```ts
export interface RailVertex { lat: number; lon: number; tunnel: boolean; }

export interface RailLine {
  id: string;            // "1" | "2" ÔÇö matches livetrains line + LINE_RGB in TrainLayer
  name: string;          // "1 Line"
  path: RailVertex[];    // ONE ordered, continuous polyline terminusÔåÆterminus
  stationIdx: number[];  // indices into `path` where stations sit (ordered)
}

export const RAIL_LINES: RailLine[] = /* generated */;
```

Two design decisions worth stating. First, the underground flag lives **per vertex**, not per
segment: a segment (the span between vertex `i` and `i+1`) is "in tunnel" iff *both* endpoints are
flagged, which lets a portal fall cleanly on a single vertex and makes the surfaceÔåötunnel boundary a
property of the geometry rather than a separate table to keep in sync. Second, stations are recorded
as **indices into the same `path`**, not as separate lat/lon. This is the load-bearing change for
underground tracking: it means an underground station (Beacon Hill, the DSTT platforms, U-District,
Roosevelt) has an exact, ordered arc-length position on the line even though no OSM "station" node
ever produced a usable above-ground coordinate. The existing flat `RAIL_STATIONS` can be kept and
re-derived from `RAIL_LINES` (`path[idx]` for each `stationIdx`) so `RailLayer`'s station bloom code
keeps working unchanged.

Keep `RAIL_SEGMENTS` exported during the transition (regenerate it from the above-ground spans of
`RAIL_LINES` so the current `RailLayer` rendering path is untouched until ┬º3 replaces it).

### 2.2 Arc-length is the coordinate system, not lat/lon

Everything underground is reasoned about in **arc-length `s`** (meters from the line's first vertex),
because that is the only coordinate that stays meaningful when GPS is gone. The shared `path.ts`
module (V4 Batch 2a, net-new ÔÇö confirmed not to exist yet) builds, per `RailLine`, a cumulative
arc-length table over `path` and exposes:

- `cumLen(line) ÔåÆ number[]` ÔÇö cumulative meters at each vertex (compute once, memoize like
  `trains.ts` does with its `CUM` `WeakMap`). Use haversine, same formula already in `trains.ts`.
- `project(line, lat, lon) ÔåÆ { s, segIdx, dist }` ÔÇö nearest point on the polyline to a fix, returning
  arc-length `s`, which segment, and the perpendicular distance (used as a confidence gate).
- `posAt(line, s) ÔåÆ { lat, lon, tunnel, heading }` ÔÇö inverse: arc-length ÔåÆ lat/lon, the `tunnel`
  flag interpolated as "true if the surrounding segment is tunnel," plus the local tangent heading
  (for the railcar glyph in Batch 2b).
- `stationWindow(line, s) ÔåÆ { prevIdx, nextIdx, sPrev, sNext }` ÔÇö the two stations bracketing `s`,
  with their arc-lengths (the unit the timetable pacer in ┬º4 works in).

This is the same arc-length machinery `trains.ts` already implements inline (`cum`, `posAt`); ┬º4's
job is to lift it into `path.ts` so live trains and sim trains share one code path.

### 2.3 Generating the tunnel-inclusive polyline in `get-rail-osm.ps1`

The fix is to stop discarding tunnels and to stitch ways in order using the OSM **route relations**
(V4 Batch 5). Concretely, change the Overpass query and the element loop:

1. **Query the route relations, not loose ways.** Sound Transit Link is modelled in OSM as
   `route=light_rail` / `route=subway` relations (1 Line and 2 Line each have one or two directional
   relations). Replace the bare `way["railway"="light_rail"]` capture with a relation capture plus
   its members and their geometry:

   ```
   [out:json][timeout:180];
   relation["route"="light_rail"]["ref"~"^[12] Line$"]($bbox);
   out body;
   >;
   out geom;
   ```

   `out body` gives the relation's ordered member list; `>;` then `out geom` pulls every member way
   with coordinates. The relation's member order is what stitches the disconnected ways ÔÇö including
   the tunnel ways ÔÇö into one terminusÔåÆterminus sequence.

2. **Keep tunnel ways; flag them.** Delete the `if ($el.tags.tunnel -eq "yes") { continue }` line.
   Instead, when emitting each way's vertices, tag them with the way's `tunnel`/`layer` state:
   `way.tags.tunnel -eq "yes"` (also treat `way.tags.layer` < 0 as a fallback) ÔåÆ `tunnel:true`.

3. **Walk the relation in order and concatenate.** For each route relation, iterate its members in
   the order OSM lists them, look up each member way's geometry, reverse a way if its endpoint
   doesn't match the running tail (standard relation-stitching: compare the way's first/last node to
   the current end and flip if needed), drop the duplicated shared vertex at each join, and append.
   The result is one ordered `RailVertex[]` per line. Emit it as `RAIL_LINES[n].path`.

4. **Snap stations onto the path.** For each `railway=station` node captured in the bbox that belongs
   to the line, find the nearest vertex (or nearest point, then insert a vertex) and record its index
   in `stationIdx`. Underground platforms that lack good surface nodes get their position from the
   relation's `stop`/`platform` member roles if present, else are hand-placed once (see ┬º5 risks) and
   snapped to arc-length. Order `stationIdx` ascending so it reads terminusÔåÆterminus.

OSM relation stitching is fiddly (V4 flags this as the medium risk of Batch 5): member ways can be
listed out of geometric order, can need reversing, and occasionally have small coordinate gaps at
joins. The reverse-and-snap walk above handles the first two; for gaps, emit a straight connector
vertex pair and flag it `tunnel:true` (a gap is almost always a bored segment OSM left implicit). The
script should print, per line, the vertex count and the tunnel-flagged fraction so a regression is
obvious at generation time.

### 2.4 Mapping a live train onto the line with stale/absent GPS

This is the heart of the coordinate fix. A vehicle is no longer "an eased lat/lon"; it carries an
**arc-length state `s` on a known line**. Per vehicle, track: `s` (current arc-length), `sVel`
(meters/sec, the pace), `line`, `lastFixAt`, and the perpendicular `dist` of the last accepted fix.

- **On a fresh, trustworthy fix** (OBA `predicted && phase=="in_progress"`, which `rail.go` already
  enforces, *and* `project().dist` below a gate, say 60 m, so a spurious off-line fix can't yank the
  train sideways): snap `s` toward `project(line, lat, lon).s`. Don't hard-set it ÔÇö ease `s` toward
  the projected value with a short time-constant (~1.5 s, the spirit of the existing `POS_TAU`) so
  re-acquisition at a portal glides instead of popping. A fix whose `dist` exceeds the gate is
  ignored for position but still refreshes `lastFixAt` (the train is alive, just off-geometry).

- **Between fixes / underground** (no fresh target, or `posAt(line, s).tunnel` is true): stop easing
  toward a stale point entirely. Instead **dead-reckon**: advance `s += sVel * dt`, where `sVel` is
  set by the timetable pacer (┬º4), not by raw GPS speed. The train keeps moving along the true tunnel
  alignment at schedule pace, which is exactly what a rider expects to see.

- **Direction.** Derive travel direction once from the sign of the last two accepted fixes' `s`
  (increasing = toward the high-index terminus) and hold it; OBA's `tripId` (`100479` vs `2LINE`,
  already parsed in `rail.go`) plus the headsign also disambiguates and should be plumbed through if
  cheap. Heading for the glyph comes from `posAt().heading`, never from lat/lon deltas of a frozen
  point.

- **Resurfacing.** When a fix re-acquires after a tunnel, `project()` snaps `s` to the surface
  alignment. Because the train was dead-reckoned to roughly the right `s` already, the correction is
  small and the ease absorbs it ÔÇö no teleport. If dead-reckoning drifted (a train held longer than
  scheduled), the correction is larger but still a smooth slide along the line, not a jump across the
  map.

- **Fade semantics fixed.** The `STALE_S`/`DROP_S` fade in `livetrains.ts` must no longer fire merely
  because GPS went quiet. Gate it on *expected* coverage: if `posAt(line, s).tunnel` is true, suppress
  the staleness fade ÔÇö a tunnel-silent train is healthy. Only fade when the train is *above ground*
  and still hasn't reported past a generous window (e.g. 2ÔÇô3├ù the segment's scheduled time), which is
  the real "the feed died" signal.

## 3. Animation grammar underground

The calm vocabulary already in the codebase is: a jade line (`RailLayer` `LINE = rgba(40,225,170)`),
lateness-as-color-temperature (`TrainLayer` `lateTint` ÔÇö warm coral late, ice blue early), station
hub bloom (the `prox`-driven halo and the one-shot arrival ring), a solid bright-cored bead for a
*measured* live train versus a hollow desaturated bead for a *scheduled* guess, and an absolute ban
on strobe or size-pulse on an always-on screen. The underground treatment must read as a *quieter
state of the same train*, not a new symbol ÔÇö the distinction is "confirmed by GPS" versus "inferred
from schedule," which the codebase already expresses as **solid-cored versus hollow/desaturated**.
We extend that existing axis rather than inventing a new one.

### 3.1 The subsurface track

Underground segments of the line should be drawn, but as a *recessed* version of the surface ribbon,
so the eye understands the train is following a real path it just can't quite see. In a new
`RailLayer` pass (or a dedicated `RailTunnelLayer` drawn *beneath* the trains and the surface line),
stroke each tunnel span of `RAIL_LINES[*].path` as a **dashed, dimmed jade hairline** ÔÇö the same hue
(`rgba(40,225,170)`) at roughly a third the alpha of the surface body, with a `setLineDash` pattern
(e.g. `[6, 7]` scaled by the existing `wm` zoom factor) and *no* glow under-stroke (the bloom is what
makes the surface line read as "lit infrastructure"; removing it makes the tunnel read as "below the
surface"). This reuses the existing `ensureProjected` view-cache mechanism ÔÇö just split the per-view
projected points into surface and tunnel sub-lists by the vertex `tunnel` flag.

### 3.2 The train while submerged

While `posAt(line, s).tunnel` is true, the bead transforms from the measured grammar to a *ghost* of
itself, paced by timetable:

- **Hollow + dimmed core.** Drop the bright near-white measured core (`rgba(232,246,255)` in
  `TrainLayer`) and render the bead as a hollow ring in the line color ÔÇö i.e. reuse the *exact*
  treatment `TrainLayer` already uses for simulated/scheduled trains (`// hollow ring (no filled
  core) marks "scheduled, not live"`). This is the key semantic move: underground, the train *is*
  schedule-paced, so it should wear the schedule-paced costume. No new symbol to learn.
- **Subdued, not faded-to-death.** Multiply the bead alpha by a fixed `~0.6` "submerged" factor (a
  constant, not the time-based `fade` ramp, which we disabled for tunnels in ┬º2.4). It dims to read as
  "underground," then *holds* steady ÔÇö it must not keep fading toward zero the way a dying feed does,
  or it re-creates the ghost-bead confusion.
- **Tail collapses to a short along-track smear.** The comet tail today is drawn from the lagging
  `alat/alon` anchor. Underground, draw the tail by walking *backward along the polyline* a fixed arc
  length (e.g. 120 m via `posAt(line, s - 120)`), so the tail hugs the tunnel curve instead of
  cutting a chord. Keep it short and low-alpha.
- **Lateness color survives.** `lateTint` still applies ÔÇö the warm/cool punctuality temperature is
  the one live signal we genuinely retain underground (OBA's `scheduleDeviation` keeps updating even
  when position doesn't, and the timetable pacer in ┬º4 can carry the last known deviation forward).
  This is a nice property: even ghosted, the train still tells you if it's running hot.
- **No pulse, no strobe.** Motion comes *only* from `s` advancing smoothly along the dashed tunnel
  track. The along-track shimmer proposed for the surface railcar glyph (Batch 2b) is omitted
  underground ÔÇö a submerged train is calmer than a surfaced one, which is the correct emotional read.

### 3.3 Station and portal transitions

The boundaries are where "doesn't make sense" most needs to become "obviously right":

- **Portal (surfaceÔåÆtunnel).** As `s` crosses a vertex where `tunnel` flips true, cross-fade the bead
  over ~0.6 s from solid-measured to hollow-submerged (core alpha ÔåÆ 0, submerged dim factor in),
  and cross-fade the track it sits on from glowing surface ribbon to dashed subsurface hairline. The
  cross-fade is a property of position (`s` near the portal vertex), so it's frame-rate independent
  and reverses cleanly when a train exits.
- **Underground station arrival.** Because stations are now `stationIdx` arc-lengths on the path, the
  existing station bloom + one-shot arrival ring in `RailLayer` fire correctly for *underground*
  stations too ÔÇö the `near` proximity test should switch from lat/lon distance to **arc-length
  distance** (`|s_train ÔêÆ s_station|`) so a train passing under Beacon Hill blooms Beacon Hill, not
  whatever surface station is closest on the map. This single change makes the underground stations
  feel alive: hubs in the tunnel breathe through the day exactly like surface hubs. The arrival ring
  is the perfect "the train is here even though you can't see it" affordance ÔÇö it's already a "bell of
  light, like a drop in water," which reads beautifully as a train surfacing into awareness.
- **Resurfacing (tunnelÔåÆsurface).** The reverse cross-fade, plus ÔÇö if a GPS re-acquisition lands at
  the same moment ÔÇö let the small `s` snap from ┬º2.4 happen *during* the cross-fade so the visual
  "the train comes back into focus" and "the position gets confirmed" are one gesture, not two.

## 4. Timetable-paced motion (the math, and where it lives)

The goal George stated and V4 Batch 3.3 records: motion that is **smooth** (no jitter between sparse
polls) *and* **schedule-accurate** (it arrives at the next station when the timetable says), with live
fixes correcting position but the *pace between fixes* coming from the schedule. All of this lives in
`path.ts` (shared) so live trains (`livetrains.ts`/`TrainLayer.ts`) and the existing sim
(`trains.ts`) converge on one pacer.

### 4.1 The pacing model

Work entirely in arc-length `s`. Each line has, per inter-station segment, a **scheduled duration**
`¤ä(prevStation ÔåÆ nextStation)` in seconds. Source these from the published Link timetable as a
per-segment table on `RailLine` (mirroring how `trains.ts` already encodes `runtime` + `dayparts`,
but at station-pair granularity so tunnels ÔÇö which are whole segments ÔÇö get their own honest
duration). Given a train at arc-length `s` between stations `A` (at `sPrev`) and `B` (at `sNext`),
its scheduled fractional progress through the segment is `f = (s ÔêÆ sPrev) / (sNext ÔêÆ sPrev)`, and the
**timetable pace** is

```
sVel = (sNext ÔêÆ sPrev) / ¤ä(AÔåÆB)      // meters per second the schedule implies
```

So the dead-reckoning advance from ┬º2.4, `s += sVel * dt`, moves the train along the segment so it
will reach `B` exactly `¤ä(AÔåÆB)` after it left `A` ÔÇö schedule-accurate by construction. A small
ease-in/ease-out (a smoothstep on `f`) at the segment ends gives the dwell-and-accelerate feel of a
real train without any pulse; this is the same smoothstep shape `trains.ts` could adopt for its
chord interpolation.

### 4.2 Blending live fixes with the pace

The two inputs combine as **a correction (position) plus a rate (pace)**:

- **Pace (`sVel`)** always comes from the timetable (┬º4.1). This is what makes the GPS gap smooth and
  schedule-true: with zero fixes for two minutes in a tunnel, the train still advances at exactly the
  scheduled rate and shows up at the next platform on time.
- **Position correction (`s`)** comes from live fixes when trustworthy. On an accepted fix, compute
  `sFix = project(line, lat, lon).s` and ease the live `s` toward it:
  `s += (sFix ÔêÆ s) * (1 ÔêÆ exp(ÔêÆdt / ¤ä_corr))` with `¤ä_corr Ôëê 1.5 s` ÔÇö the same exponential-smoothing
  form `livetrains.ts` uses today, just applied to arc-length instead of raw lat/lon. Between fixes
  this term is inert and pure timetable pacing carries the train.

Optionally bias the *pace* by OBA's `scheduleDeviation`: if a train is reported 90 s late, stretch
the remaining segment durations slightly so the dead-reckoned ghost doesn't run ahead of a train the
agency already knows is behind. A simple version: scale `sVel` by `clamp(remainingScheduled /
(remainingScheduled + devSec), ÔÇª)`. This keeps the underground ghost honest about lateness without
needing a position fix.

### 4.3 Where the code lives

`path.ts` (Batch 2a) owns the geometry primitives (`cumLen`, `project`, `posAt`, `stationWindow`,
plus a `paceVel(line, s)` and an `advance(state, dt, fixes)` reducer). `livetrains.ts` changes from
"store eased lat/lon" to "store `{line, s, sVel, lastFixAt, devSec}` and call `advance` each tick";
its `liveTrains()` accessor returns `posAt(line, s)` lat/lon + the `tunnel` flag + a `submerged`
boolean so `TrainLayer` can pick the surface vs ghost grammar. `trains.ts`'s inline `cum`/`posAt`
get deleted in favor of `path.ts` so the simulator and the live pacer are the *same* arc-length
engine ÔÇö which is what finally lets a scheduled 1 Line train glide through the DSTT correctly even
before the GPS key produces a single underground-adjacent fix.

## 5. Implementation plan (ordered, file-by-file)

Dependencies first: this work sits on V4 **Batch 5** (the `RAIL_LINES` polyline) and **Batch 2a**
(`path.ts`). Do them in this order; each step is independently verifiable on the Pi gate.

1. **`pi-setup/get-rail-osm.ps1` ÔÇö generate `RAIL_LINES` (Batch 5).** Switch the Overpass query to
   route relations, stop discarding tunnel ways, stitch members in order with reverse-and-snap, flag
   each vertex's `tunnel` state, snap stations to `stationIdx`, and emit `RAIL_LINES` (keep
   `RAIL_SEGMENTS`/`RAIL_STATIONS` derived for back-compat). Run on the PC (needs internet), commit
   the regenerated `rail.ts`. *Verify:* per-line vertex/tunnel-fraction print at generation; the line
   rendered by the existing `RailLayer` still matches reality above ground.

2. **`rail.ts` types.** Add `RailVertex` / `RailLine` / `RAIL_LINES` exports (mechanically produced
   by step 1; this step is just locking the TS interface the rest of the code imports).

3. **`web/src/display/render/path.ts` ÔÇö new shared module (Batch 2a).** Implement `cumLen`,
   `project`, `posAt`, `stationWindow`, `paceVel`, and the `advance(state, dt, fixes)` reducer over a
   `RailLine`. Pure functions, no rendering. *Verify:* a small node/TS unit test (the Cowork sandbox
   can run TS even though it can't build Go) ÔÇö round-trip `s ÔåÆ posAt ÔåÆ project ÔåÆ s`, and check a
   dead-reckoned `s` reaches a station at its scheduled time.

4. **Timetable data on `RailLine`.** Add the per-station-pair scheduled durations `¤ä` (and reuse the
   `dayparts` headway shape from `trains.ts`). Source from the published Link schedule. This is the
   one piece that needs **real-world data** (see risks).

5. **`internal/feed/rail.go` ÔÇö optional plumbing.** Forward OBA `tripId`/headsign so the client can
   pin direction without inferring it from two fixes. Low priority; the client can infer direction
   without it. No change to the 20 s poll or the snapshot shape otherwise.

6. **`web/src/display/render/livetrains.ts` ÔÇö arc-length state.** Replace the eased-lat/lon `Veh`
   with `{line, s, sVel, devSec, lastFixAt, dist}`. On each poll, gate fixes by `project().dist`, ease
   `s` toward `sFix`. In `tickLiveTrains`, call `path.advance` (timetable pace + dead-reckon).
   Suppress the staleness fade while `posAt(s).tunnel` is true. `liveTrains()` returns lat/lon (from
   `posAt`) + `submerged` + `heading` + the surviving `devSec` and `fade`. *Verify:* simulate a poll
   gap (drop fixes for 2 min) and confirm `s` keeps advancing on schedule.

7. **`web/src/display/render/TrainLayer.ts` ÔÇö surface vs ghost grammar.** Branch on `submerged`:
   above ground, the existing solid measured bead; underground, the hollow dimmed ring with the fixed
   submerged-alpha, the along-track (polyline-walked) short tail, and `lateTint` retained. Cross-fade
   at portals on `s` proximity to a tunnel-flip vertex.

8. **`web/src/display/render/RailLayer.ts` (or new `RailTunnelLayer`) ÔÇö subsurface track.** Split the
   projected path into surface and tunnel sub-lists by vertex flag; stroke tunnel spans as the dashed
   dimmed hairline with no glow, drawn beneath the trains. Switch the station-proximity bloom/ring
   test from lat/lon distance to arc-length distance so underground stations bloom correctly.

9. **`web/src/display/render/trains.ts` ÔÇö converge on `path.ts`.** Delete the inline `cum`/`posAt`;
   drive the simulator through `path.ts` over `RAIL_LINES` so scheduled trains follow the *real*
   alignment (including tunnels) instead of straight station chords. Extend the sim to the 1 Line now
   that it has complete tunnel-inclusive geometry (currently deferred in `trains.ts` for exactly this
   gap).

### Risks and real-world data needs

- **OSM relation stitching (medium, flagged in V4).** Member ways out of order, needing reversal, or
  with small join gaps. Mitigated by the reverse-and-snap walk and gap-as-tunnel-connector fallback,
  but the generated line must be eyeballed against the real alignment after step 1. If the OSM
  relations for Link are incomplete in the bbox, fall back to capturing tunnel ways by `tunnel=yes`
  and ordering them by nearest-endpoint chaining.
- **Tunnel station coordinates (real data).** Underground platforms (Beacon Hill, DSTT:
  Westlake/Symphony/Pioneer Square/Int'l District, U-District, Roosevelt) may lack clean OSM nodes.
  Where the relation's `stop`/`platform` members don't supply coordinates, hand-place once against a
  known reference and snap to arc-length; these are static and rarely change.
- **Published timetable durations (real data).** The per-segment `¤ä` table must come from the actual
  Link schedule (the underground segments especially ÔÇö DSTT dwell + run times). Without it the pacer
  falls back to distributing the line `runtime` by arc-length, which is smooth but not
  schedule-accurate through the tunnel. Treat the real per-segment table as the upgrade that makes
  underground motion *true*, the way the live GPS key is the upgrade that makes surface motion
  *confirmed*.
- **Direction inference (low).** Until `tripId` is plumbed (step 5), direction comes from two
  accepted fixes; a train that enters a tunnel immediately after its first-ever fix has ambiguous
  direction for one segment. Acceptable ÔÇö it resolves on the next surface fix, and the timetable pace
  keeps it moving sensibly meanwhile.

This sequence kills the three failure modes from ┬º1 at their root: ordered tunnel geometry removes
the chord-shortcut and wrong-place surfacing, arc-length dead-reckoning removes the freeze, and the
tunnel-gated fade removes the false ghosting ÔÇö while the train wears a calm, recognizable
"schedule-paced underground" costume that the codebase's existing hollow-bead grammar already taught
the viewer to read.
